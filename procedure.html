Experimental Procedure

Detailed methodology for testing skin contact effects on metal-acid reactions

Experimental Design

We tested three conditions for each metal (Zinc, Steel, Magnesium) to determine how skin contact affects reaction rates with 0.5 M dilute hydrochloric acid.

Condition 1: Cleaned Metal

Metal thoroughly washed with deionized water and ethanol to remove all surface contaminants

Expected: Fastest reaction

Step-by-Step Procedure

Step 1

Safety Preparation

Put on safety goggles, lab coat, and ensure work area is well-ventilated. Review HCl handling procedures. Place absorbent paper on work surface.

Step 2

Sample Preparation

Obtain metal samples (Zinc, Steel, Magnesium). For cleaned condition: wash with deionized water and ethanol. For untouched: handle only with gloves. For skin contact: touch with bare hands for 30 seconds.

‚è±Ô∏è 10 minutes
Step 3

Acid Preparation

Measure 20 mL of 0.5 M dilute HCl into a beaker using a pipette. Ensure consistent volume across all trials for accurate comparison.

‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
Step 4

Reaction Timing

Place metal sample into acid. Immediately start stopwatch. Observe for signs of reaction (bubbling, heat, color change). Record time when fizzing stops or visibly slows.

‚è±Ô∏è Variable (observe reaction)
Step 5

Data Recording

Record reaction time, metal type, condition tested, temperature change if measured, and qualitative observations (color, intensity of bubbling, etc.).

‚è±Ô∏è 3 minutes
Step 6

Repetition & Cleanup

Repeat steps 2-5 for all three conditions and all three metals. After each test, rinse beaker thoroughly. Dispose of residual acid safely. Clean and dry equipment before next trial.

Variables Controlled

Independent Variable

Type of surface condition: Cleaned, untouched (gloved), or skin contact

Dependent Variable

Reaction time: Measured in seconds until reaction significantly slows

Constants

Metal type tested per trial, HCl concentration (0.5 M), volume of acid (20 mL), temperature, metal sample size
