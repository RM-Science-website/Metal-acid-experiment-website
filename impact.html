üß¥ Skin Oils Form a Barrier

The oils on human skin (sebum) are hydrophobic and contain complex organic molecules. They create a physical and chemical barrier on the metal surface that interferes with the acid's direct contact with reactive metal.

üßÇ Salts & Ions from Skin

Human skin secretes salt ions (sodium chloride, potassium) and other electrolytes. These modify the electrochemical properties of the metal surface, affecting how quickly the acid can attack the metal.

‚ö° Altered Surface Chemistry

The combination of oils and salts changes the metal's surface chemistry, disrupting the normal oxide film dissolution and creating a less favorable environment for the acid-metal reaction to proceed rapidly.

‚ú® Clean Surfaces React Fastest

When metals are cleaned with sandpaper, we remove all surface contaminants, exposing bare reactive metal. This allows maximum acid-metal contact and fastest reaction rates‚Äîas we observed in our data.
